**The Impact of Carbon Policy News on the National Energy Industry**  
Hugo Morão (2023)

> This paper studies the impact of unexpected carbon policy shocks on the Portuguese energy sector using a proxy Structural Vector Autoregression (SVAR). Exploiting exogenous variation from regulatory announcements, the analysis finds that tighter carbon policies increase domestic turnover, labor demand, and prices, suggesting that firms adjust through investment and cost pass-through mechanisms rather than contraction.

Empirically, the endogenity problem. Carbon prices, energy demand, and macro conditions move together. Morão addresses this using **high-frequency policy shocks**—unexpected regulatory announcements—and embeds them in a **Bayesian SVAR with an external instrument**. 

- **Exogenous EU Carbon Surprises**  
 Känzig's series that logs 126 exact days when the European Union surprised the market with new carbon regulations.

- **Domestic Boom**  
  Domestic turnover, wages(8%) and hours(5%) worked immediately increase. Because companies rapidly upgrade their infrastructure and invest in low-carbon tech. 

- **Prices adjust quickly and materially**  
  Domestic output prices increase by up to 6% within three months, passing through to consumers. 

Carbon pricing drives the green transition by forcing domestic investment, creating jobs, and raising wages. But also acts as a short-term inflationary shock for consumers, which governments must be prepared for.

## Electric Bicycles and Public Transport Tickets: Ownership and Car Use Patterns  
**Nils Christian Hoenow, Viola Helmers, Eva H. Yang (RWI, 2024)**

If you thought subsidizing e-bikes was the silver bullet for reducing car use, this paper delivers a strong reality check.

> "Does buying an e-bike actually make you drive less?"

Using a large German dataset and propensity score matching (PSM), the authors uncover a stark contrast between user groups.

### Key Findings

- **E-bike owners:**
  - Older, wealthier, rural men
  - Treat e-bikes as a **complement** to cars
  - Minor short-term reductions in driving
  - **No change in annual car mileage**

- **Public transport ticket holders:**
  - Younger, urban women
  - Use transit as a **substitute** for cars
  - **~4,200 km reduction per year**

---

## Takeaway: Fund Infrastructure

- E-bike subsidies → **limited impact on decarbonization**
- Public transit investment → **substantial reduction in car use**

> If governments are serious about reducing emissions,  
> they should invest in systems that actually replace cars.

**Real modal shift comes from public transport, not e-bikes.**
